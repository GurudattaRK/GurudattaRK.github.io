About

Systems programmer with a fascination for what happens at the hardware boundary.

I am a systems programmer based in India, currently working at Qualys on macOS agent security and Software Composition Analysis. Before that, I interned at Sentinel One doing reverse engineering and detection rule optimization for macOS malware.

My real interest lies at the boundary between software and hardware — x86-64 microarchitecture, cache hierarchies, branch prediction, and the art of making code run as fast as the silicon allows. I built PicoPerf to answer not just how long a code path takes, but why — by isolating cache pressure, branch mispredictions, and IPC degradation as independent hardware signals.

Through Low-Latency-Lab, I rebuild my earlier projects with modern HPC techniques — io_uring async I/O, AVX2 SIMD, lock-free MPMC queues, O_DIRECT zero-copy disk I/O, multi-process worker pools with CPU pinning, and PGO compilation. The work involves deep perf profiling to identify bottlenecks at the instruction and cache-line level, then systematically eliminating them — from removing SQPOLL kernel-thread contention to cutting 48% of wasted spin-loop instructions.

When I am not writing assembly or staring at perf stat output, I am reading about physics, mathematics, or philosophy — usually at 3am.
